Eleven Eleven {1111} Journal of Literature and Art was founded in 2004 by Youmna Chlala and Gayle Romasanta during their MFA in Writing at California College of the Arts. The vision of the journal was to create a forum for risk and experimentation in both writing and visual art. It developed an international presence while creating interdisciplinary opportunities within CCA. Youmna Chlala served as the editor until 2007. Subsequent editors have included Brent Foster Jones, Ben Lerner, Hugh Steinberg and Joseph Lease.


11.11 is now published once a year by the MFA Writing Program at California College of the Arts, through a process where we gather our admired colleagues and pals to share some words. The spare, open pages, and the brevity of offerings are meant to allow the reader the feeling of reading, across the wide, white page, “cover to cover.” The idea is to invite readers and writers into a digital town square of poetics, where the conversation is meant to be casual, exciting, nourishing and inspiring – as though you ran into a smart friend, sat down for a coffee, and they shared their thoughts across forms. We also hope it offers the really great feeling that you have read it all, cover to cover.
